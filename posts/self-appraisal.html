One of the most important skills that you need to develop is to clearly document your contributions and accomplishments in the self appraisal document during a performance review. This is always considered a boring chore. But you can convert this into an opportunity to showcase your value, map your career trajectory and reflect on your growth opportunities. Let us look at some of the key details that must be included in the self appraisal document. This is generic guidance, but can easily be adapted to employees of any level or role.

Business Impact

Instead of just listing features that you worked on, you have to shift the narrative to what the business impact of your work was. Don't just record your activities, but also record how your contributions affected user behavior, product adoption or business metrics. Try to assign a quantifiable value to the impact in terms of revenue, cost savings, user satisfaction, product performance, or any other metric that is relevant to your business.

Example
  • Weak: "I significantly improved the performance of the landing page."
  • Strong: "Reduced landing page latency by 30%, resulting in a 2% increase in conversion rate."

If you are looking to add structure to this section, you can adopt the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) or Google's XYZ formula.

Knowledge Sharing

You are expected to share your knowledge with your team members and stakeholders. This can be in the form of documentation, mentoring, or technical guidance. This contributes to the overall productivity and capability of your team in the long term. A lot of times this can be invisible, but you have to explicitly call out all the instances where you shared your knowledge and how it helped the team. This helps in building the likelihood of you being viewed as a domain or product or a technical expert, which will lead to more higher-value opportunities.

Collaboration Success

At any level in your organization or in any role, you will be expected to collaborate with your team members and stakeholders in other teams and external organizations. This can be in the form of technical discussions, projects with shared goals, exploration of greenfield opportunities, or resolving client issues which need contributions from multiple teams or direct interfacing with clients or their representatives.

You should write about the decisions you influenced, the risks you reduced, how you helped accelerate the timelines, and resolved the conflicts. In summary, you should write about your impact on the collaboration and not just participation. Call out the people, teams and organizations that you collaborated with and the success it led to.

Promotion

If you are working towards a promotion in the short term, you will have to state the job responsibilities of the next level that you successfully executed. Your management is going to promote you only when you are consistently demonstrating the job responsibilities of the next level. This means that you are aware of such responsibilities and consciously taking them up and successfully executing them to demonstrate your readiness for the next level. Another factor that is not in our control for the promotion is the need for another person at the next level, which also needs to be considered. At the same time, you can tilt the situation in your favour by asserting that you would be filling that business need, rather than just waiting for it to happen.

Trainings and Corporate Expectations

There are always a number of trainings and corporate expectations that you need to complete every year. This is more of a checklist where you need to state that you completed all of them. This will demonstrate your timeliness and diligence in performing these tasks on a regular basis.

Goals and Alignment

At the beginning of the year, you should ensure that you are having a discussion with your management to gain a clear understanding of the goals and expectations for the year for your role and level. At frequent junctures, maybe once a quarter, you must discuss with your management whether you are aligned and on track to achieve the goals. You must also check if your work is closely aligned with the roadmap of your product and organization.

During these check-ins, you must talk about what you need to operate at the top of your abilities and skills to successfully execute the goals. It also helps to maintain a brag document so that you don't forget these details by the time of the performance review.

What is a Brag Document?

A brag document is a running log where you record your accomplishments, impact, and praise in real-time throughout the year. This makes it much easier to write your self-appraisal when the time comes.

Growth Opportunities

A truly great self-appraisal isn't just a brag sheet. It is also an opportunity to reflect on your areas for growth and improvement. You can write the areas where you fell short and the steps you are taking to improve them. You can include them into your goals and future trainings to make the self-improvement action oriented. This builds immense trust with your manager and shows high self-awareness.

Communication

Use direct and concise language to write your self appraisal. Again, avoid using adjectives or adverbs that do not provide a clear measure of your performance. Keep the content short and use quantitative values to highlight your accomplishments. You do not have to create a long laundry list of your activities, instead focus on the ones which had the highest visible impact across your team and organization.

Use this as an opportunity to highlight any hidden or invisible contributions that have directly contributed to the success of your product and organization. A single metric is more persuasive than a paragraph of adjectives. Let the data do the talking. Keep the content grounded to reality and do not overstate your accomplishments using language that is not backed by data or metrics.

Tip

Instead of "I was responsible for..." or "I helped with...", use powerful action verbs like Spearheaded, Optimized, Orchestrated, or Automated when they accurately reflect your role.

Invest time in your self appraisal document and do not rush it as a last minute administrative task. Consider it as a prominent tool that helps in your career growth. Ensure that it is comprehensive, accurate and impactful. Take a proactive approach on your goals at the beginning of the year and consistently work towards them through the year. Let the self appraisal document serve as a scorecard at the end of the year to reflect on your achievements and areas for improvement. Writing a strong self appraisal document is the most effective way to own your career narrative.
