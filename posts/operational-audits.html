Software lives in operations longer than in development. So it is prudent to optimize for being more effective and efficient in software operations.

DevOps is an organizational principle where the same development team is responsible for the entire product lifecycle, from development to operations.

The following is a list of checks that the product owning team can audit on a regular cadence to ensure that they are set up for being successful in operations.

Alerts

  • Alerts are configured for all relevant metrics for the product.
  • There are engineers assigned on rotation to acknowledge the alerts and respond to them.
  • A secondary support engineer is assigned to respond to the alerts in case the primary personnel is unavailable. This ensures redundancy and facilitates knowledge transfer through shadowing.
  • Alerts that do not result in action are continuously removed to reduce alert fatigue.

Monitoring

  • Dashboards exist for all relevant Service Level Indicators (SLIs) and Service Level Objectives (SLOs) for the product.
  • The dashboards are actively reviewed by the support engineers during on-call hours.
  • SLIs and SLOs are properly identified and defined accurately for your products. They are removed or updated as the product evolves.

On-call Support

  • A proper on-call roster is available for daily, weekend and holidays for at least the next three months.
  • The on-call roster is publicly available for everyone to refer to, including engineer's contact details.
  • The on-call notification system is configured with the correct escalation path.
  • The time between the incident and the on-call response must be minimal and reduced over time. The team is responsible for capturing and reporting this response time.
  • A handoff meeting should be conducted when on-call responsibility is passed on to another engineer, focusing on a summary of issues and learnings.

Incident Management

  • The time gap between the incident occurrence and detection must be minimal and reduced over time. The team is responsible for capturing and reporting this time to detection.
  • The team works on restoring the service immediately and restoration of service is prioritised over investigation of the defect/issue in the system.
  • The Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR) must be captured and reported for all service unavailability issues and incidents. The team must actively strive to reduce MTTR.
  • Clearly documented step by step playbooks are in place and used for resolving incidents.
  • The incident tracker tool is updated on time with relevant details.
  • A post-mortem report is created and corrective/preventive action items are identified. These action items are fed back into the product backlog for execution. Post-mortems are blameless and focus on system and process improvements rather than individual fault.

Ops Reviews

  • A weekly report of the SLIs, SLOs and resource utilization of the product is presented to the team by the support engineer.
  • Action items are identified during the Ops Reviews and are owned by the support engineer. They are tracked using a tracking system and status reported for all open and in-progress items during subsequent Ops reviews.
  • Reports (possibly based on the tracking system) are available to view all the corrections based on Ops Reviews.

Production Issues

  • Production issues are investigated and resolved based on priority.
  • Reports (possibly based on the tracking system) are available to measure timeliness of response on support issues.
  • Engineers are sufficiently trained to execute responsibilities during on-call and incidents. The engineers on support must be given explicit training for handling sensitive information.
  • The team maintains a defined training module for new joiners for performing on-call and resolving incidents.
  • Engineers have sufficient accesses and resources (documentation and playbooks) for performing on-call and resolving incidents.

Deployment

  • Deployments are automated and repeatable, with manual steps minimized and documented when unavoidable.
  • There exists a checklist for deployments thoroughly documented and used for all deployments by the deployment engineer.
  • A rollback playbook exists and is used for all deployments that fail.

Operations should be a continuous process of self evaluation and improvement. The team should strongly inculcate a culture of ownership and accountability for the operations of their product, not just its development.
